Moisturizers: Oil-free moisturizers like Tatcha The Water Cream provide lightweight hydration without adding to the skin's oiliness. Gentle Cleansers: Use fragrance-free, non-comedogenic cleansers, such as oil-based or milky cleansers, which remove impurities without stripping natural oils. Examples include oil cleansers paired with a damp microfiber cloth or milky oil cleansers. Humectants: Look for ingredients like hyaluronic acid, glycerin, panthenol (vitamin B5), and lactic acid, which attract water to the skin from the environment or deeper layers, helping to hydrate dry skin. Emollients: These ingredients soften and moisturize the skin by replenishing lipids. Common emollients include ceramides, shea butter, cocoa butter, jojoba oil, sunflower seed oil, rosehip oil, and squalane. Occlusives: These create a physical barrier on the skin to prevent moisture loss. Effective occlusives include petrolatum, mineral oil, beeswax, argan oil, and dimethicone. Moisturizers: Use a rich moisturizer twice daily. Day creams should ideally include broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher to protect against sun damage, which can worsen dryness. Night creams can be heavier and contain ingredients like niacinamide to support barrier repair and hydration. Face Oils: For very dry skin, applying a face oil as the final step in your nighttime routine can help lock in moisture. Serums and Treatments: Hydrating serums containing hyaluronic acid and ceramides are beneficial. Alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs) like lactic acid can gently exfoliate dead skin cells, improving texture and allowing better absorption of moisturizers, but should be used cautiously. Body Care: Use body creams or soufflés with plant butters, oils, and ceramides to hydrate skin from the neck down. Products with shea butter and glycerin are particularly effective. Lifestyle Practices: Limit showers to 5–10 minutes with lukewarm water, avoid harsh soaps, and apply moisturizer to damp skin to seal in hydration.
